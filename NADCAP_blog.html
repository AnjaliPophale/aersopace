NADCAP Certification: What It Takes and Why It Matters in Aerospace Manufacturing

1. NADCAP Certification: What It Takes and Why It Matters in Aerospace Manufacturing

In aerospace manufacturing, precision is not just a goal—it's a mandate. Components must meet the most stringent quality, safety, and compliance standards to ensure performance in some of the most extreme environments imaginable. One of the key certifications that validates such standards is NADCAP.
But what exactly is NADCAP certification, what does it take to achieve it, and why is it considered the gold standard in the aerospace and defense sectors?
Let's break it down.

What is NADCAP Certification?

NADCAP stands for National Aerospace and Defense Contractors Accreditation Program. It's a global cooperative accreditation program administered by the Performance Review Institute (PRI) and created by major aerospace OEMs and suppliers.
It is not a generic quality certification like ISO—it is an industry-specific, process-focused certification that audits special processes critical to aerospace and defense manufacturing. These include:

  • Non-Destructive Testing (NDT) – FPI, MPI, UT, RT
  • Welding (TIG, resistance, etc.)
  • Heat treatment
  • Surface enhancement (peening, coatings)
  • Chemical processing
  • Composites and more

What It Takes to Get NADCAP Certified

Achieving NADCAP certification is a rigorous, multi-step process involving:

  1. Pre-Assessment & Gap Analysis
    Companies begin by reviewing their processes against NADCAP checklists. This helps identify compliance gaps, training needs, or documentation issues.
  2. Internal Process Standardization
    Every process—be it welding, NDT, or surface treatment—must be documented, repeatable, and traceable. Operators and inspectors must be qualified. Calibration, quality control, and materials handling must meet NADCAP specs.
  3. Formal Audit by PRI
    A PRI-approved auditor visits your facility for a full technical assessment. The audit includes:
    • On-floor process reviews
    • Personnel qualification checks
    • Procedure reviews
    • Test reports and traceability validation
  4. Non-Conformance Management
    Any non-conformities raised during the audit must be addressed with corrective and preventive actions (CAPA). The quality of the root-cause analysis plays a major role in the final decision.
  5. Accreditation and Ongoing Surveillance
    Once certified, companies must maintain continuous compliance and are re-audited periodically to ensure consistent process quality.

Why NADCAP Certification Matters

  • 1. Global Recognition & Customer Trust
    NADCAP is recognized by aerospace giants like Boeing, Airbus, GE Aviation, Rolls-Royce, Safran, and Lockheed Martin. Having this accreditation places your company in a league of highly trusted global suppliers.
  • 2. Entry into Strategic Supply Chains
    Many OEMs mandate NADCAP certification before allowing vendors to bid or supply for critical components. It's a key to unlocking business with Tier 1 aerospace leaders.
  • 3. Process Excellence & Product Reliability
    The NADCAP framework enforces a culture of process control, documentation discipline, and operator skill, ensuring high repeatability and zero-defect deliveries.
  • 4. Competitive Differentiator
    In a competitive manufacturing landscape, NADCAP certification sets you apart. It shows you're not just meeting industry standards—you're helping shape them.
  • 5. Reduced Risk, Higher Accountability
    With detailed audits, traceability, and operator accountability, the risk of part failure, recall, or reputational damage is significantly reduced.
